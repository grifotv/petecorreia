For the past 6 years, I’ve been working in the various parts of complex digital products. I’ve amassed a broad range of skills in the process — user interface design, front-end development, user experience and identity design.

I believe that when you want to come up with the best solution, learning all disciplines that are involved in making digital products allows a more holistic approach to solving a problem — and not to mention better communication with the different specialised teams.

Recently, I’ve spearheaded the product design and front-end development of a couple of award-winning Internet of Things products, which got featured in international publications.

Thingful 2013 — 15

Thingful is the world's first search engine for the Internet of Things, providing a unique geographical index of connected objects around the world, including energy, radiation, weather, and air quality devices as well as seismographs, iBeacons, ships, aircraft and even animal trackers. It also enables people and companies to claim and verify ownership of their things using a provenance mechanism, among other features.

I helped define the product, designed the identity and user interface, and also single-handedly developed the front-end of the application, which works across multiple devices.

Xively 2011 — 13

Xively by LogMeIn is the industry’s first public cloud, purpose-built for developing and managing commercial products on the Internet of Things. It simplifies the interconnection of devices, data, people and places, accelerating the creation of compelling connected products.

I led the Design/Front-end team on the full rebrand and redesign as Xively — complete with a new identity, marketing collateral and fully responsive user interface across the entire platform and satellite products.

Umbrellium 2013 — 15

Umbrellium creates and commercialises participatory products and services that empower people to transform their cities. The company designs, engineers and distributes everything from hardware products to software systems and the infrastructures that unite the two.

I designed the identity of the company, and also designed and developed interactive art applications.
